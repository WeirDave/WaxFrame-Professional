  - HIVE-SIZE SWEEP — RUN B-REPEAT (3-AI, 2nd run) logged. CONFIRMS the
    3-AI bump is REAL, not the antigravity fluke. Clean 3-AI (ChatGPT
    builder + Claude + Gemini): 8 raw -> 5 REVIEW rounds (3 BO), 802s
    engaged (13.4 min), UNANIMOUS convergence, 5 USER DECISIONS
    (R2/R4/R6), final 77w/461c, same 216w draft.
  - ★ HEADLINE FINDING — THE CURVE IS A HUMP AT 3, NOT MONOTONIC.
    Engaged time by hive size:
        2-AI (A):    9.7 min            4 review   (unanimous)
        3-AI (B):    12  min            6 review   (majority)
        3-AI (B2):   13.4 min           5 review   (unanimous)
        4-AI (C):    8.5 min            4 review
        5-AI (D):    6.8 min            4 review
        6-AI (E):    6.0 min            3 review
    Three AIs is REPRODUCIBLY the SLOWEST configuration — slower than 2,
    and slower than 4/5/6 — across TWO runs (12 and 13.4 min) with very
    different decision loads (2 vs 5), so it is NOT a decision artifact.
    3-AI is the only size run twice, making it the strongest signal here.
  - MECHANISM (hypothesis): the majority-convergence engine has an
    unstable middle at 3. 2 AIs = tight unanimity loop that settles.
    4-6 AIs = STABLE majority (3-of-4 / 4-of-6); one contrarian can't
    move the doc and "no changes" votes pile up fast. At 3, majority is
    2-of-3: a single active dissenter keeps proposing changes, the
    Builder keeps applying them, the doc keeps shifting, and the other
    two keep getting re-un-satisfied -> oscillation. Fragile majority +
    one holdout = grind. Not enough mass to stabilize, too much to stay
    tight.
  - PRODUCT TAKEAWAY (pending replication of 2/4/6): run 2 AIs (cheap,
    fine) or 4+ AIs (fastest). 3 is the worst of both worlds. Counter-
    intuitive and marketable once replicated. Reframes David's original
    "fewer is slower" hunch: half-right — 3 is the actual trap.
  - REPLICATION STATUS: 3-AI = n=2 (confirmed). 2/4/5/6-AI = n=1 each.
    Next for rigor: repeat 2-AI, 4-AI, 6-AI to confirm the hump shape
    (that 2<3 and 4<3 hold up). The 3-AI peak itself is already solid.
  - SIDE NOTES: decision count is NOT size-determined (3-AI runs had 2
    and 5 decisions) — it's run-to-run variance, which is exactly why
    the review-rounds (BO-stripped) metric and replication matter.
    B-repeat's final was 77w (vs 40-56w elsewhere) — the 3-AI grind
    also trimmed LESS aggressively / oscillated more on content.

       moves review-rounds-to-converge (reviewers run in parallel).
       "More = faster" NOT supported on rounds; but "more = slower" also
       NOT supported. Leaning toward: size is roughly neutral on review
       rounds in the 2-5 band.

  • Two of three sub-sections (TripAdvisor and Yelp) landed at template

    TripAdvisor's 500–900 word range. TripAdvisor compressed at 1.74×

platform. Three reasons in priority order:
  1. Length positioning — combined converges at the floor; split sits
     naturally mid-range.

     before Builder-Only; split needed 2.
  3. Native voice per platform — split outputs don't carry artifacts
     of being part of a triptych.
