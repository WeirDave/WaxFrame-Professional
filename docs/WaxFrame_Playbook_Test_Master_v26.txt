        Review template, v3.51.0 Pro). REFERENCE BASELINE
        for split-vs-combined comparison. 6 rounds / 35 min
        total (R3 outlier at 33.5 min — likely rate-limit /
        slow responder; productive time without R3 ~14 min).
        Three sub-sections: TripAdvisor 510w (2% of range,
        AT floor), Google Maps 180w / 1,045c (66% of range,
        mid-zone), Yelp 339w (10% of range, near floor).
        Confirmed the structural problem with combined
        templates: AIs default to MINIMUM length across all
        sub-constraints to avoid violating any one of them.
        Two of three sections landed at template floor.

      • Manly Bikes Yelp v1.0 (Rewrite as Yelp, v3.52.5 Pro).
        8 rounds / 21 min. Final 542w / 3,142c (60% of
        300–700w range, SWEET SPOT — vs combined-Yelp's
        10% of range). Majority-convergence engine triggered
        at R8 with holdouts present. First DeepDive run with
        v3.52.4 round+role attribution live (56 captures
        bucketed cleanly by R1–R8 and worker/builder). DeepSeek
        slow-responder dropped after R1; David switched
        Builder via Change Builder modal; run completed
        cleanly. Validation of v3.49.0 builder-disable
        recovery + v3.52.4 attribution + v3.52.5 source-size
        check (901-word source pasted, oversized card fired).

    ARCHITECTURAL CONCLUSION (from this comparison): split
    templates outperform combined templates by every metric.
    Per-section length positioning is the smoking gun —
    combined-template AIs converge at the floor across all
    constraints; split-template AIs sit naturally in the
    middle of their own range. Locked in backlog v28
    "Items shipped this session" + this file.
